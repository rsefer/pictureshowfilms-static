TigerHawk

Nike

In advance of Nike's deal to become the NFL's official uniform provider during the 2012 season, Picture Show (via production partner Core 12) was asked to produce a piece as part of an internal campaign for Nike's international entities that told the story of the importance of football in American culture. 'TigerHawk' focuses on one meeting of two perennial Chicago-area high school powerhouse programs, the Wheaton South Tigers and the Maine South Hawks. In the excerpt above, Wheaton South running back Dan Vitale talks about the thrill of playing under the lights on Friday night and the responsibility he carries every time he touches the football.

The Catholicism Project

National Public Television

Word on Fire, a not-for-profit global Catholic media organization headed by Fr. Robert Barron, has teamed with Picture Show to produce and direct a ten part documentary series on the history of Catholicism. Syndicated nationwide on public television through WTTW in Chicago, the series explores, through a global journey, the living culture of the Catholic faith: from the lands of the Bible, to the great shrines of Europe, to the shores and heartland of America, to the mysteries of Asia, to the rich landscapes of Latin America, to the heart of Africa and beyond.

"A.V. Undercover" Series Trailer

The A.V. Club

Picture Show has been producing the A.V. Club's flagship content series 'A.V. Undercover' since its inception in 2010. Unlike other music performance series, 'A.V. Undercover' has a bit of a game element in the concept: Each year (we're now in the midst of the third season), the A.V Club creates a list of 25 songs and invites bands into a small, round room located in their office to perform covers of them. Once a song gets chosen, it gets crossed off the list. So, the later a band comes in, the fewer songs they have to choose from.

Mercedes-Benz "After Market Loyalty" Campaign

Core 12, LLC

Picture Show, teaming with interactive partner Core 12, LLC, was tasked with creating a series on the Mercedes-Benz dealer service experience for use in a series of regional campaigns. Shooting on location at Mercedes-Benz of Chicago, Picture Show crafted the Mercedes-Benz story during the course of a normal workdays, capturing the unscripted responses of customers and employees to tell the stories of value, quality and experience brand promise for one of the auto industry’s power names.

CDW: Configuration Center

Ogilvy & Mather

CDW boasts one of the most advanced configuration centers on North America. The speed and accuracy with which CDW can implement specific software and hardware specifications and have them to their clients, ready to use out of the box, is a major differentiator for them in the technology industry. This piece, part of the "People Who Get It" campaign by Ogilvy & Mather, goes inside one of their configuration centers to feature both the scale of the facility and the technicians working at the desks inside of it.

The Museum of Science & Industry (excerpt)

Hoffman-York

The Museum of Science and Industry in Chicago is a true civic institution. Housed in the only remaining structure from the Columbian Exposition of 1893, MSI has operated as a world-class museum for over 75 years. With the introduction of their new Science Storms wing, MSI underwent a complete rebranding. Picture Show was selected to create a piece that not only paid homage to what the museum has accomplished over its celebrated history, but also to showcase the exhibits and programs that are propelling the brand as a vanguard amongst science museums worldwide. For the campaign, Picture Show created 30 second broadcast spots, a fundraising/external marketing feature as well as the spot excerpted here, used to introduce the rebranding and new museum mission to current and future employees.

Lettuce Entertain You: The First 40 Years (Excerpt)

Lettuce Entertain You

Begun in 1971 by partners Rich Melman and Jerry Orzoff, Lettuce Entertain You has grown from a one restaurant, shoestring organization into a legendary Chicago business success story. Proprietors of dozens of successful restaurant brands, Lettuce specializes in everything from lunchtime takeout to Michelin-starred fine dining restaurants. The requirement given by Lettuce after we were hired to direct and produce this story (amongst a field of several other candidates) was to create a piece that was unlike most corporate productions. They wanted the passion, commitment, creativity and humor that has defined their company's success to be evident to the viewer. Our unscripted production approach allowed us to do just that as we captured the perspectives and personalities of those at the top levels of the organization down to those who wash the dishes.

ALMAA Identity Piece

EPIC / ALMAA

ALMAA (Advocacy, Living and Mentoring After Abuse) is a group that provides education and resources to prevent sexual abuse. Produced in conjunction with EPIC (http://iamepic.org/), a company that builds pro bono creative teams for nonprofits, the goal of the piece was to not only define the need for the organization, but also to tell the stories of the personal experience ALMAA's founders had with surviving abuse themselves.

The Real Life End (Excerpt)

Sony / BMG

Prior to its release, Sony/BMG came to Picture Show in need of DVD content for the deluxe edition of the Grammy-nominated ‘Once’ soundtrack. In the spirit of the Academy Award winning film, “The Real Life End” is an intimate look at the lives of film’s stars, Glen Hansard and Marketa Irglova. Our one-person crew captured the incredible story of how an inspired filmmaker and his friends in Dublin overcame a litany of obstacles to make a little film that became an international success.

Cowboys Stadium is the largest domed stadium on earth, featuring the world's largest column-free interior. Its also one of the most technologically advanced stadiums ever built and that technology was put in place by CDW. This piece was produced as part of Ogilvy & Mather's "People Who Get It" campaign.

The Ride of Our Lives

National Public Television Series

When Picture Show’s Mike Leonard made the hasty decision to pack three generations of family into two rented RVs and hit the road for a month-long cross country adventure, he had no idea that the journey would lead to a best-selling book, The Ride of Our Lives, and a critically acclaimed, groundbreaking television series of the same name. In a media age of fake reality and celebrity worship, The Ride of Our Lives swings the pendulum back to what is truly authentic. More than a journey across America, it’s a ride through life.

Fathers & Sons

Public Television Feature

Lary Sorensen is a former Major League Baseball All Star who, at a very young age, achieved success on a level most can only dream about. But that success exacted a major toll, both personally and professionally, leaving Lary facing an uncertain future both financially and with his family.

Thirty years after Lary was drafted into professional baseball, his son Mark finds himself on the same precipice, a touted major league prospect on the verge of breaking into the pro ranks. He inherited a major league pedigree, but will Lary’s past and uncertain future affect Mark’s chances of realizing his own big league dreams?
