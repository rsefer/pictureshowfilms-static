    Our friend Al passed away suddenly on Sunday in Nashville. Despite any formal schooling, Al was an astute businessman who was fueled by the revolutionary idea that artists should own and be the primary beneficiaries of the music they create. He took great risks for what he believed in and stands as an exemplar of how to succeed in a creative business without compromising artistic merit. Al could also tell a great story, a skill perhaps honed from the fact that his gregarious nature and sharp wit resulted in life experiences that deserved constant re-examination. So, it's with great sadness as we sit and think about him today that we'll never again get to hear, in Al's inimitable way, about the psychological damage he incurred as a child getting cursed out by ventriloquist dummy Jerry Mahoney or about the time he got chased out of the White House by Lady Bird Johnson or about the pleasure he took in stealing Billy Bob Thornton's toupée. He was a tough guy with a generous heart. He never picked up the lunch tab, but he would give you the shirt off his back if you needed it. The phrase 'one-of-a-kind' gets thrown around a lot in remembrances, and I feel like attaching that cliché to Al does him a disservice. But, to try and describe him any other way leaves me at a loss for words, one of the few things I don't think Al ever experienced during his time on earth. http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6509492/al-bunetta-longtime-manager-of-john-prine-dies-at-72
