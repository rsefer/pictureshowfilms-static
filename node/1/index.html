Who we are

Storytelling is an art. Used for thousands of years, from sketches on cave walls to high definition screens, the medium has evolved but the essence of good storytelling remains the same. A good story can inspire enlighten & entertain. It can stick with you forever.

We are creative storytellers that use the art of the moving picture to inspire feeling and forge an authentic connection with our audience.

We make small stories universal, and the larger-than-life stories deeply personal. We strive to be compelling and entertaining. We believe in witty, smart humor when it’s called for. We do not manufacture emotion &  believe that nothing is as compelling as what’s real.

Jamie Fleischel
Partner

Jamie decided he wanted to pursue a career in production after spending five weeks in a van, on tour with an American band in Europe. His only possessions the trip were a couple changes of clothes and a Hi-8 video camera that his parents had just given him for Christmas. To the chagrin of his traveling companions, Jamie shot several hours of footage on the trip. Upon returning home, he used that footage to begin the process of learning how to edit, becoming somewhat obsessed with making movies in the process.

Since then, Mr. Fleischel (as he prefers to be called) has had the opportunity to work with and direct Grammy winners and Oscar winners, create pieces for some of the world’s most well-respected brands and appear on national television wearing a fake mustache.

Matt Leonard 
Partner

Matt Leonard began his creative trajectory at a young age when he submitted a video instead of a book report for Mrs. Lincoln’s eighth grade class. Despite dubiously casting himself as a ferocious boxer, the video earned him an “A”, which caused Matt to correctly predict that making these little movies would help him get out of writing papers for years to come.

After graduating from Providence College, Matt taught acting classes, teaching newcomers how to be comfortable in front of an audience as well as a camera. The height of Matt’s own acting career was appearing in a local television commercial as a bagboy for a grocery store chain, suggesting that maybe he was better suited to be behind the camera.

In recent years, Matt has been around the world directing shoots in Europe, Africa, Mexico, the Middle East and from various corners of North America. His directing and editing work has been featured prominently on national broadcast outlets and, perhaps in an effort to prove there’s nothing he can’t do,
Matt has voiced several national TV and
radio spots.
